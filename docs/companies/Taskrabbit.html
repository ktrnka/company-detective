About Taskrabbit

Taskrabbit, Inc. is an American online marketplace that connects individuals with local freelancers, known as "Taskers," who provide various services. The company was founded in 2008 by Leah Busque and initially operated under the name RunMyErrand before rebranding to Taskrabbit in 2010. In 2017, Taskrabbit was acquired by an affiliate of IKEA, which has significantly influenced its growth and service offerings (Wikipedia, 2012).

Taskrabbit operates in over 75 cities across eight countries, with a network of more than 200,000 independent Taskers as of 2024 (Wikipedia, 2012). The company has transitioned to a remote-first model, allowing employees to work flexibly while maintaining hubs in key locations such as San Francisco, Philadelphia, and London (BuiltInSF, 2024).

Taskrabbit's platform allows users to hire Taskers for a variety of services, including handyman services, moving assistance, furniture assembly, cleaning, and personal assistant tasks. The platform also supports virtual tasks, such as data entry and guidance via video calls, emphasizing safety and flexibility, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic (Taskrabbit, 2024).

The company primarily generates revenue through service fees charged to Taskers, which can be as high as 70% of the original bid for tasks (Wikipedia, 2012). Taskrabbit serves a diverse customer base, including individuals needing help with everyday tasks and small businesses seeking assistance with various operational needs. The platform is particularly beneficial for those who require immediate or same-day services (Taskrabbit, 2024).

Taskrabbit has experienced substantial growth, particularly during the COVID-19 pandemic, which accelerated the demand for gig economy services. The company has also expanded its service offerings through strategic partnerships and acquisitions, such as the acquisition of Dolly, a moving services company, in November 2024 (Wikipedia, 2012).

Key Personnel

Ania Smith has been the CEO of Taskrabbit since August 2020. She brings extensive experience from her previous roles at companies like Airbnb and Uber. Under her leadership, Taskrabbit has focused on optimizing its marketplace dynamics, ensuring a balance between the supply of Taskers and the demand for services (Forbes, 2024).

Partnerships and Acquisitions

In September 2024, Taskrabbit announced a partnership with Cachet, a company specializing in personalized insurance for gig workers. This collaboration aims to provide Taskers with a custom insurance package that offers financial security solutions, including coverage for income loss due to accidents and long-term disability (Cachet, 2024).

In November 2024, Taskrabbit acquired Dolly, a moving services company based in Seattle. This acquisition is expected to expand Taskrabbit's service offerings while maintaining the distinct identities of both brands (Wikipedia, 2012).
