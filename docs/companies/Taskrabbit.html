About Taskrabbit

Taskrabbit is a marketplace platform founded on January 1, 2008, by Leah Busque. It connects individuals with local freelancers, known as "Taskers," who provide various home-related services. The company was acquired by the Ingka Group (IKEA) in 2017, which has facilitated its expansion and integration with IKEA's offerings, particularly in furniture assembly and home improvement tasks (Crunchbase, 2024).

Taskrabbit operates in thousands of cities across nine countries, including Canada, France, Monaco, Germany, Italy, Portugal, Spain, the United Kingdom, and the United States. It is also available in over 200 IKEA stores worldwide (Crunchbase, 2024). The platform serves a diverse customer base, primarily individuals seeking help with household tasks, moving, and personal errands. Taskrabbit is a B2C company, facilitating connections between clients and Taskers through its online marketplace and mobile application.

The company has raised a total of $37.8 million in funding across seven rounds, with the latest being a Series C round in July 2012 (TaskRabbit Company Overview, 2021). While current revenue figures are undisclosed, the integration with IKEA is expected to enhance its financial performance through increased service demand.

Taskrabbit's services include handyman services, moving assistance, furniture assembly, cleaning, lawn care, plumbing, grocery shopping, and more. The platform allows for same-day bookings, depending on Tasker availability (TaskRabbit Services, 2021).

Key Personnel

Ania Smith is the current CEO of Taskrabbit. She has emphasized the importance of flexibility and autonomy for Taskers, especially in the evolving gig economy. Smith has noted that the pandemic accelerated the acceptance and growth of marketplace companies, as more people seek to outsource tasks to free up time for personal interests (Taskrabbit CEO Talks About The Evolution Of Gig Economy Companies, 2024).
