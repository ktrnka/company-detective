About Akili Interactive

Akili Interactive is a digital medicine company that integrates neuroscience with technology and video game entertainment to create digital therapeutics. Founded in 2011 by Eddie Martucci, the company is headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts. Akili's flagship product, EndeavorRx, is the first prescription treatment delivered through a video game, designed to improve attention function in children with ADHD. The company also offers EndeavorOTC, a treatment option for adults with ADHD, expanding its reach to a broader audience (Akili Interactive).

Akili's business model primarily targets children with ADHD and their families, as well as healthcare providers who prescribe EndeavorRx as part of a treatment plan. The company also engages with adults through EndeavorOTC. In 2023, Akili reported revenues of $1.7 million, with $523,000 attributed to EndeavorRx and $1.2 million from EndeavorOTC. Despite these revenues, the company faced a net loss of $59.5 million, leading to significant workforce reductions and a reevaluation of its business model (Digital therapeutics firm Akili to sell for $34M, MedTech Dive, 2024-05-30).

Akili's products are distributed through healthcare providers and are part of a comprehensive therapeutic program that may include medication and therapy. The company has also expanded its market reach through a partnership with Shionogi & Co. to introduce a localized version of EndeavorRx in Japan (Akili Announces Positive Results from Shionogi‚Äôs Phase 3 Clinical Trial, BioSpace, 2024-02-26).

Over time, Akili has faced challenges in building a sustainable business model, leading to a shift from a prescription model to over-the-counter offerings. In May 2024, Akili announced its acquisition by Virtual Therapeutics for approximately $34 million, marking a significant shift in its business strategy (Digital therapeutics firm Akili to sell for $34M, MedTech Dive, 2024-05-30).

News

Acquisition and Strategic Shifts

In May 2024, Akili Interactive was acquired by Virtual Therapeutics for approximately $34 million. This acquisition is part of a strategic shift to create a larger digital health firm focused on mental health solutions through immersive gaming experiences (Digital therapeutics firm Akili to sell for $34M, MedTech Dive, 2024-05-30). Prior to the acquisition, Akili faced challenges in building a sustainable business model, leading to a shift from a prescription model to over-the-counter offerings and significant workforce reductions (Akili to lay off 46% of its staff, STAT News, 2024-04-30).

Product Developments and Market Expansion

EndeavorRx, Akili's flagship product, is a digital therapeutic designed to improve attention function in children aged 8 to 17 diagnosed with ADHD. Clinical studies have shown significant improvements in attention, with 73% of children reporting improved attention compared to 50% in the control group (What to Know About EndeavorRX, CNET, 2023-12-24). Akili is also expanding its market reach through a partnership with Shionogi & Co. to introduce a localized version of EndeavorRx in Japan, which has shown promising results in clinical trials (Akili Announces Positive Results from Shionogi‚Äôs Phase 3 Clinical Trial, BioSpace, 2024-02-26).

Financial Performance

In 2023, Akili reported revenues of $1.7 million, with $523,000 attributed to EndeavorRx and $1.2 million from its over-the-counter version, EndeavorOTC. Despite these revenues, the company faced a net loss of $59.5 million, prompting significant workforce reductions and a reevaluation of its business model (Digital therapeutics firm Akili to sell for $34M, MedTech Dive, 2024-05-30).

Akili Interactive is at a critical juncture, navigating the challenges of the digital therapeutics market while seeking to leverage its innovative products like EndeavorRx and EndeavorOTC. The recent acquisition by Virtual Therapeutics may provide the necessary resources and strategic direction to enhance its offerings and expand its market presence.
