About Imagine Pediatrics

Imagine Pediatrics is a virtual healthcare provider based in Nashville, Tennessee, specializing in pediatric care for children with complex medical needs. The company offers 24/7 support through a team of healthcare professionals, including doctors, nurses, social workers, therapists, and pharmacists. Their services are designed to provide continuous care and reduce the need for frequent hospital visits. Imagine Pediatrics primarily serves children covered by Medicaid Managed Care Organization (MCO) plans, offering their services at no cost to these families (Imagine Pediatrics, Date Unknown).

The company was founded by Alison Curfman, Meghan Haycraft, Taylor Beery, and Zeena Johar, with George Boghos currently serving as CEO (Tracxn, 2023-10-11). While specific historical details about the company's founding date are not provided, Imagine Pediatrics has established itself as a vital resource for families navigating complex healthcare needs for their children.

Imagine Pediatrics generates revenue through partnerships with Medicaid MCOs, providing services to children with complex, chronic medical conditions, frequent hospitalizations, and multiple medications and specialists. The company's services are distributed through a mobile app and phone calls, ensuring accessibility for families in need.

The company's primary product is its virtual healthcare services, which include care coordination, timely access to therapy, and medication management. Imagine Pediatrics has demonstrated significant impacts through its interventions, such as coordinating referrals for speech therapy and cardiology, ensuring continuity of care, and streamlining medication orders (Imagine Pediatrics, Date Unknown).

Future Prospects

As a relatively new player in the virtual healthcare space, Imagine Pediatrics has not yet made any acquisitions or investments. The increasing demand for telehealth services, particularly in pediatrics, suggests that the company could expand its offerings and market reach in the coming years (Tracxn, 2023-10-11).
