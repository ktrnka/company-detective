About Verkada Inc

Verkada Inc. is a cloud-based security technology company founded in 2016 by Filip Kaliszan, Hans Robertson, James Ren, and Benjamin Bercovitz. The company is headquartered in San Mateo, California, and has grown rapidly since its inception. Verkada specializes in providing integrated security solutions, including video cameras, access control systems, environmental sensors, alarms, and intercoms, all managed through a single cloud-based platform (Verkada Company Overview, 2023).

As of October 2023, Verkada has raised over $460 million in funding and employs more than 2,100 people across 15 offices worldwide. The company serves over 28,000 customers in more than 70 countries, indicating a significant global presence (Wikipedia, 2020) (Technical Support Engineering Intern - Fall 2025 - Verkada Inc, 2023). Verkada's customers include educational institutions, law enforcement agencies, and commercial enterprises, highlighting its B2B focus (Verkada Company Overview, 2023).

Verkada's products are distributed through a combination of direct sales and partnerships, allowing them to reach a wide range of clients. The company has been described as a leader in the security technology space, leveraging cloud-based solutions to provide comprehensive security systems that are scalable and user-friendly (Verkada Company Overview, 2023).

Key Personnel

  • Filip Kaliszan: CEO and co-founder, with a background in computer science from Stanford University. He previously co-founded CourseRank, which was sold to Chegg.com.
  • Hans Robertson: Executive Chairman and co-founder, previously COO of Meraki, with degrees from MIT. Meraki was acquired by Cisco for $1.2 billion.
  • James Ren: Co-founder and software architect, with experience at Google and Chegg, holding degrees from Stanford and a JD from Harvard.
  • Benjamin Bercovitz: Co-founder, former CTO of CourseRank, with degrees from Stanford (Verkada Company Overview, 2023).

News

FTC Settlement and Data Breach

In March 2021, Verkada experienced a significant data breach where hackers accessed live feeds and archived videos from its security cameras. This breach raised concerns about the company's data security practices, particularly as it involved sensitive locations like psychiatric hospitals and schools (Verkada Settles FTC Probe of Hack Exposing Tesla, Hospitals, 2024). Following the breach, Verkada implemented several security measures, including mandatory two-factor authentication for support staff and increased transparency with customers regarding security practices (Wikipedia, 2020).

On August 30, 2024, the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) announced a proposed settlement with Verkada, requiring the company to pay a $2.95 million penalty and implement a comprehensive information security program. This settlement addresses allegations that Verkada failed to secure customer data adequately, allowing hackers to access sensitive information, including live feeds from over 150,000 security cameras in various sensitive locations such as hospitals and schools (FTC Takes Action Against Security Camera Firm Verkada, 2024) (DOJ and FTC Secure $2.95 Million Settlement for Alleged CAN-SPAM Act Violations, 2024).

New Product Developments

Verkada has been actively expanding its product offerings. Recently, the company introduced AI-powered alerts and next-generation cameras, intercoms, and door readers at its annual event, VerkadaOne. Additionally, Verkada achieved FedRAMP Ready status at the Moderate Impact Level, indicating its commitment to meeting federal security standards (Verkada by Public Affairs, 2024).

Financial Performance and Valuation

Verkada's valuation has seen significant growth, with a reported valuation of $3.2 billion following a $205 million Series D funding round in September 2022. As of December 2024, the company is reportedly raising $200 million at a valuation of $4.5 billion, reflecting a substantial increase in investor confidence despite the recent regulatory challenges (Wikipedia, 2020) (Security Camera Startup Verkada in Talks for $4.5 Billion Valuation, 2024).

Leadership and Company Culture

Verkada has faced criticism regarding its workplace culture, particularly allegations of a "bro" culture and inadequate security practices. In response, Verkada has committed to improving its workplace environment and addressing gender pay equity (Wikipedia, 2020).

Partnerships and Community Engagement

Verkada has partnered with organizations like Safe and Sound Schools to enhance safety measures in educational institutions. The company has also engaged in community initiatives, such as donating security cameras to Asian Pacific American business communities to combat rising violence against these groups (Verkada by Public Affairs, 2024).
