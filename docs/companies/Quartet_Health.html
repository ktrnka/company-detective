About Quartet Health

Quartet Health is a behavioral healthcare company founded on January 1, 2014, and is based in New York, United States (Crunchbase, 2025). The company focuses on providing value-based behavioral healthcare, aiming to improve access to high-quality care for individuals with behavioral health needs, including serious mental illness (SMI). Quartet Health operates in 39 states and partners with health plans, health systems, and community mental health centers to deliver comprehensive behavioral health solutions (Quartet Health on 2024-09-20).

Quartet Health's services include care enablement and delivery, connecting patients with high-quality providers, and collaborating with digital care partners like Brightside for online mental health care. They offer comprehensive treatment plans that include medication and therapy, with appointments available within 48 hours (Digital Care Partners on 2021-12-17).

The company primarily operates in a B2B model, serving health plans, health systems, and community mental health centers. Quartet Health's revenue model is based on partnerships with these entities to provide behavioral healthcare solutions (Clover Health Investments, Corp, Clover Health Investments, 2024).

Quartet Health has raised a total of $265 million in funding, with significant investments from Oak HC/FT, GV (formerly Google Ventures), and Centene Corporation (VBC Provider Operations Manager Job, 2024). The company has grown through strategic acquisitions, such as the purchase of innovaTel, a telepsychiatry provider, in 2021 (Network Growth Specialist - Quartet Health, 2024).

Key Personnel

Christina Mainelli is the CEO of Quartet Health. She emphasizes the importance of addressing the interconnectedness of physical and mental health needs for individuals living with SMI (Quartet Health, 2024). Dr. Michael J. Lipp serves as the Chief Medical Officer, focusing on improving patient access to coordinated care (Clover Health Investments, Corp, Clover Health Investments, 2024).

Recent Developments

New Product Launch

On March 19, 2024, Quartet Health launched the Whole Health program in partnership with Clover Health. This program is the first fully integrated physical and behavioral health model that takes accountability for the total cost of care (TCOC) for health plans' most complex members (Quartet Health, 2024). The program aims to provide comprehensive care that is coordinated and convenient, addressing the unique needs of underserved populations (Clover Health Investments, Corp, Clover Health Investments, 2024).

Partnership with Clover Health

Quartet Health's partnership with Clover Health is a strategic move to enhance care delivery for Clover's members suffering from SMI. Quartet's expert care teams will augment Clover Home Care's capabilities, providing in-home care access, medication support, and mental and physical care coordination (Clover Health Investments, Corp, Clover Health Investments, 2024).

Acquisition News

In January 2025, Iris Telehealth acquired innovaTel from Quartet Health to enhance its telepsychiatry services (Chris Larson, 2025-01-21). This acquisition reflects the ongoing consolidation and strategic realignment within the telehealth sector.

Partnerships

In October 2024, Quartet Health partnered with Arbital Health to increase value-based mental healthcare, further expanding its network and capabilities (Mobi Health News, 2024-10-17).

Conclusion

Quartet Health is a prominent player in the behavioral healthcare industry, focusing on value-based care and integrated health solutions. With strong leadership, strategic partnerships, and substantial financial backing, the company is well-positioned for continued growth and impact in the healthcare sector. The recent launch of the Whole Health program and partnerships with organizations like Clover Health and Arbital Health highlight Quartet Health's commitment to improving behavioral health outcomes through innovative and comprehensive care models.
