About Pomelo Care

Pomelo Care is a healthcare technology company founded on January 1, 2021, and is headquartered in Jersey City, New Jersey. The company specializes in providing virtual maternity and neonatal care services, aiming to improve maternal and newborn health outcomes through a combination of telemedicine and in-person support. Pomelo Care partners with health plans and employers to offer comprehensive support for pregnancy and newborn care, including 24/7 access to care teams via text, phone, or video consultations (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20).

Pomelo Care's services are distributed primarily through partnerships with employers and health plans, making it a B2B company. The company offers its services at no cost to eligible members, enhancing the care provided by OB-GYNs and midwives with additional support and resources. As of 2024, Pomelo Care has expanded its reach to cover over 15 million lives, including more than 1 in 6 Medicaid beneficiaries nationally (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-09-24).

The company has raised a total of $86 million in funding, with its most recent round being a $46 million Series B financing led by First Round Capital and Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) (Crunchbase, 2024). Pomelo Care has grown its covered lives from 2 million to over 3 million in 2024, providing care to patients in 46 states (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20).

Pomelo Care is described by third parties as a leader in the virtual maternity care space, with a unique offering that combines virtual and in-person support, particularly following its acquisition of The Doula Network (Femtech Insider, 2024-09-24).

Key Personnel

Marta Bralic Kerns is the Founder and CEO of Pomelo Care. She has emphasized the importance of aligning incentives among patients, providers, and payors to improve health outcomes. Her leadership is focused on scaling Pomelo's care model to reach more pregnant individuals across the country (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20).

News

Funding and Financials

On June 20, 2024, Pomelo Care announced a $46 million Series B financing round, bringing its total funding to $86 million. This round was led by First Round Capital and a16z, with participation from Stripes, SV Angel, Operator Partners, and BoxGroup. The funding is intended to accelerate partnerships with payors to address the maternal health crisis in the U.S. (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20).

Acquisition of The Doula Network

On September 24, 2024, Pomelo Care announced the acquisition of The Doula Network, the largest network of credentialed doulas in the U.S. This acquisition allows Pomelo to offer a hybrid model of care that combines virtual and in-person support, significantly expanding access to maternity care across the country (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-09-24).

Clinical Outcomes and Research

Pomelo Care has published peer-reviewed data demonstrating the effectiveness of its care model in improving clinical outcomes. Research presented at the 2024 ACOG Clinical & Scientific Meeting showed that Pomelo's telemedicine care increased rates of evidence-based aspirin prophylaxis by 2.4 times and mental health screening rates by 7 times, significantly reducing risks associated with maternal morbidity and mortality (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20). Additionally, Pomelo's virtual care model has been associated with a 15% reduction in total care costs in a Medicaid population, demonstrating a 2.3:1 return on investment relative to Pomelo's fees (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-12-11).

Market Position and Competitors

Pomelo Care is positioned as a leader in the virtual maternity care space, competing with other companies like Maven Clinic, which recently raised $125 million to expand its offerings in women's health (MobiHealthNews, 2024-06-20). The acquisition of The Doula Network further strengthens Pomelo's market position by integrating in-person support into its virtual care model, a unique offering in the U.S. maternity care landscape (Femtech Insider, 2024-09-24).

In conclusion, Pomelo Care is making significant strides in addressing the maternal health crisis in the U.S. through its innovative virtual care model and recent acquisition of The Doula Network. With substantial funding and a growing network of covered lives, the company is well-positioned to expand its impact on maternal and newborn health outcomes.
