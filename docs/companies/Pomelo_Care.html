About Pomelo Care

Pomelo Care is a virtual medical practice dedicated to enhancing maternal and newborn health outcomes through a value-based care model. The company was founded in 2021 by Marta Bralic Kerns, who has a background in Medicaid and maternal health. Pomelo Care's mission is to address the maternal health crisis in the United States by providing personalized, proactive care from preconception through an infant's first year. The company partners with health plans and employers to deliver its services at no cost to patients, covering over 3 million lives across 46 states as of 2024 (Pomelo Care by Marta Bralic Kerns).

Pomelo Care has raised a total of $79 million in funding, with $33 million raised in 2023 and an additional $46 million in 2024. Major investors include Andreessen Horowitz and First Round Capital (Pomelo Care by Pomelo Care). The company has expanded its coverage from 2 million to over 3 million lives in 2024, serving patients in 46 states (Pomelo Care by Pomelo Care).

Pomelo Care offers a comprehensive virtual maternity care program that includes 24/7 access to care teams via text, phone, or video. Each patient is assigned a nurse care partner and a multidisciplinary team that may include OB-GYNs, midwives, therapists, dietitians, and lactation consultants. The company uses data-driven interventions to identify risk factors and improve care outcomes, such as increased screening rates for mental health and preeclampsia prevention (Virtual Maternity Care Program).

Pomelo Care partners with various health plans and employers, including Medicaid managed care organizations and commercial health plans. Notable partnerships include Mount Sinai Health System and Koch Industries (Pomelo Care by Pomelo Care).

Key Personnel

  • Marta Bralic Kerns: Founder and CEO of Pomelo Care. She has a background in Medicaid and maternal health, emphasizing the importance of aligning incentives among patients, providers, and payors to improve health outcomes for high-risk populations (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

  • Dr. Vineeta Agarwala: General partner at Andreessen Horowitz and a board member of Pomelo Care. She has noted the company's achievement of "true scale" in the health tech sector, evident in its extensive partnerships and the number of lives it covers (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

News

Funding and Expansion

In June 2024, Pomelo Care announced a successful Series B funding round, raising $46 million led by existing investors First Round Capital and Andreessen Horowitz (a16z). This funding will be used to scale its operations and enhance partnerships with payors across the country, aiming to address the root causes of the maternal health crisis in the U.S. The company has now raised a total of $79 million (Axios, 2024-06-20).

Acquisition and Service Expansion

Pomelo Care recently acquired The Doula Network, allowing the company to offer both virtual and in-person support. This acquisition significantly expands its service offerings and coverage to over 15 million lives, including one in six Medicaid beneficiaries (Pomelo Care, 2024-09-24).

Product and Service Enhancements

Pomelo Care's virtual maternity care platform offers personalized care plans based on individual risk factors and goals, with 24/7 access to care team members via text, phone, or video. The company's comprehensive care model integrates medical, behavioral, and social support, addressing the multifaceted needs of pregnant individuals and new parents (Pomelo Care, 2024-09-24).

Pomelo Care utilizes claims and health record data to proactively identify risk factors and implement effective interventions, leading to significant improvements in clinical outcomes. For instance, Pomelo's care model increased rates of evidence-based aspirin prophylaxis by 2.4 times and mental health screening rates by 7 times (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

Conclusion

Pomelo Care is at the forefront of addressing the maternal health crisis in the United States through its innovative virtual care model. With significant funding, strategic partnerships, and a commitment to evidence-based practices, the company is well-positioned to make a meaningful impact on maternal and newborn health outcomes. The recent acquisition of The Doula Network further enhances its service offerings, allowing for a hybrid model of care that combines virtual and in-person support, thereby expanding access to quality maternity care across the nation.
