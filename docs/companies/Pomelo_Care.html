About Pomelo Care

Pomelo Care is a virtual medical practice dedicated to improving maternal and newborn health outcomes through a value-based care model. The company was founded in January 2021 by Marta Bralic Kerns and is headquartered in New York City. Pomelo Care aims to address the maternal health crisis in the United States by providing personalized, proactive care from preconception through an infant's first year. The company partners with health plans and employers to deliver its services at no cost to patients, covering over 15 million lives across 46 states as of 2024 (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

Pomelo Care's services include 24/7 access to care teams via text, phone, or video, personalized care plans, and collaboration with in-person providers. The company also offers integrated doula services following its acquisition of The Doula Network (Pomelo Care, 2024-09-24).

Pomelo Care operates on a B2B model, partnering with Medicaid managed care organizations, commercial health plans, and employers such as Koch Industries and Mount Sinai Health System (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20). The company has raised a total of $86 million in funding, with the most recent Series B round raising $46 million in June 2024 (Crunchbase, 2024).

Key Personnel

News

Funding and Financial Growth

Pomelo Care has successfully raised $46 million in a Series B funding round announced on June 20, 2024. This round was led by existing investors First Round Capital and Andreessen Horowitz (a16z), with participation from Stripes, SV Angel, Operator Partners, and BoxGroup. The funds are intended to expand partnerships with payors and scale their care model (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

Acquisition of The Doula Network

On September 24, 2024, Pomelo Care acquired The Doula Network, the largest network of credentialed doulas in the U.S. This acquisition allows Pomelo to offer a hybrid model of care that combines virtual and in-person support, significantly expanding access to maternity care across the country. With this integration, Pomelo now covers over 15 million lives, including more than 1 in 6 Medicaid beneficiaries nationally (Pomelo Care, 2024-09-24).

Product Overview

Pomelo Care's virtual maternity care platform provides comprehensive services from preconception through the first year of an infant's life. The platform includes 24/7 access to care, personalized care teams, and evidence-based interventions. The company's care model has demonstrated significant improvements in clinical outcomes, such as a 2.4x increase in aspirin prophylaxis for preeclampsia and a 7x increase in mental health screening rates (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

Market Position and Scale

Pomelo Care has rapidly expanded its reach, growing from covering 2 million lives to over 3 million in just the first half of 2024. The company partners with major health plans and employers, including Penn Medicine and Koch Industries, to provide care across 46 states (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

Competitive Landscape

Pomelo Care ranks 4th among 269 active competitors in the maternity care sector, with a total funding of $87 million across four rounds. Its primary competitors include Maven Clinic and Babyscripts, both of which also focus on improving maternal health outcomes through technology (Tracxn, 2021-08-17).

Conclusion

Pomelo Care is at the forefront of addressing the maternal health crisis in the United States through its innovative virtual care model and recent strategic acquisition. With significant funding and a growing network of partnerships, the company is well-positioned to expand its impact on maternal and newborn health outcomes. The company's focus on addressing the root causes of maternal health disparities positions it as a leader in the healthcare industry.
