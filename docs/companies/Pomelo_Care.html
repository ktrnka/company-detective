About Pomelo Care

Pomelo Care is a virtual medical practice founded in 2021, headquartered in New York, focusing on maternal and newborn health. The company aims to address the maternal healthcare crisis in the United States by providing comprehensive, evidence-based care from preconception through an infant's first year. Pomelo Care operates on a value-based care model, offering 24/7 access to a dedicated care team that includes doctors, midwives, nurses, dietitians, and therapists (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

Pomelo Care's services are primarily B2B, targeting health plans, employers, and healthcare providers. The company collaborates with major Medicaid and commercial insurers, as well as organizations like Penn Medicine and Koch Industries, to expand access to its services (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20). The service is available at no cost to members of participating health plans or employers, and patients can check their eligibility through a quick questionnaire (Pomelo Care, n.d.).

The company has experienced significant growth, expanding its coverage from 2 million to over 3 million lives across 46 states (Axios, 2024-06-20). Pomelo Care has raised a total of $79 million in funding, with a recent $46 million Series B round in 2024, led by First Round Capital and Andreessen Horowitz (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

Key Personnel

Marta Bralic Kerns is the founder and CEO of Pomelo Care. She has been pivotal in shaping the company's vision and strategy, focusing on addressing the root causes of maternal health disparities. Her leadership has been instrumental in achieving significant milestones and driving innovation in the maternal health space (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

News

Funding and Growth

In June 2024, Pomelo Care raised $46 million in Series B funding, bringing its total funding to $79 million. This funding round was led by First Round Capital and Andreessen Horowitz, with participation from Stripes, BoxGroup, and other angel investors. The funds are intended to expand partnerships with payors and enhance the company's care model (Pomelo Care, 2024-06-20).

Acquisitions

In September 2024, Pomelo Care acquired The Doula Network, allowing the company to offer both virtual and in-person doula support, further enhancing its comprehensive care model (MobiHealthNews, 2024-09-30).

Recognition

Pomelo Care was recognized as one of Fierce Healthcare's "Fierce 15" of 2024, highlighting its innovative approach to addressing the maternal healthcare crisis (Fierce Healthcare, 2024).
