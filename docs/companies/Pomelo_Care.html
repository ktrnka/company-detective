About Pomelo Care

Pomelo Care is a New York-based virtual medical practice focused on improving maternal and newborn health outcomes through a value-based care model. Founded in 2021, the company has rapidly expanded its services and partnerships, addressing significant gaps in maternal healthcare in the United States. Pomelo Care's mission is to provide comprehensive, evidence-based care from preconception through an infant's first year, utilizing a personalized care model that includes a dedicated team of obstetricians, pediatricians, nurses, dietitians, and therapists (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, June 20, 2024).

Products and Services

Pomelo Care offers a virtual maternity care platform designed to supplement traditional in-person care provided by OB-GYNs and midwives. Key features include: - 24/7 Personalized Care: Continuous support through text, phone, or video. - Customized Care Plans: Personalized care plans addressing specific needs such as managing nausea, hypertension, and breastfeeding challenges. - Collaboration with In-Person Providers: Coordination with patients' existing healthcare providers. - Eligibility and Cost: Services are available at no cost to members of participating health plans or employers.

Business Model

Pomelo Care operates on a B2B model, partnering with health plans and employers to provide their services at no cost to eligible members. The company has demonstrated significant improvements in maternal health outcomes, including a 2.4x increase in aspirin prophylaxis for preeclampsia and a 7x increase in mental health screening rates among Medicaid patients (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, June 20, 2024).

Scale and Distribution

Pomelo Care currently covers over 3 million lives across 46 states, with plans to expand further. The company distributes its services through partnerships with health plans and employers, ensuring broad access to their virtual care platform (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, June 20, 2024).

Recent Developments

  • Funding: Pomelo Care recently raised $46 million in Series B funding, bringing total funding to $79 million. This capital will be used to expand partnerships with health plans and improve access to care (Axios, June 20, 2024).
  • Acquisition: Pomelo Care acquired The Doula Network, the largest network of credentialed doulas in the U.S., allowing them to offer both virtual and in-person care (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, September 24, 2024).

Key Personnel

Marta Bralic Kerns

Marta Bralic Kerns is the founder and CEO of Pomelo Care. She emphasizes the importance of addressing systemic issues in maternal healthcare and is committed to providing no-cost access to quality maternal and infant care. Under her leadership, Pomelo aims to significantly reduce avoidable complications and improve health outcomes (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, June 20, 2024).
