About Pomelo Care

Pomelo Care is a digital health platform that specializes in providing comprehensive maternity and neonatal care. Founded in 2020 and headquartered in New York City, the company aims to improve maternal and newborn health outcomes through a combination of virtual and in-person care. Pomelo Care's services are designed to supplement traditional in-person care from OB-GYNs and midwives, offering additional support throughout the fertility, pregnancy, and postpartum journey (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20).

Pomelo Care operates on a B2B model, partnering with health plans and employers to offer its services at no cost to members. The company covers over 3 million lives across 46 states, working with major health plans and employers to provide virtual care solutions (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20). The company's revenue model is based on these partnerships, where it collaborates with payors to improve health outcomes and reduce costs.

The company's services include a Virtual Maternity Care Program, which provides personalized care teams consisting of doctors, midwives, nurses, dietitians, therapists, and lactation consultants. These teams offer 24/7 access via text, phone, or video, and create customized care plans tailored to individual needs (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20).

Pomelo Care has raised a total of $87 million in funding, with its most recent round being a $46 million Series B financing announced in June 2024 (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20). The company has also expanded its services through the acquisition of The Doula Network, the largest network of credentialed doulas in the U.S., to offer a hybrid care model that combines virtual and in-person support (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-09-24).

Key Personnel

Marta Bralic Kerns is the founder and CEO of Pomelo Care. She has been instrumental in shaping the company's vision and strategy, focusing on aligning incentives among patients, providers, and payors to improve health outcomes for high-risk populations (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20). Kerns emphasizes the importance of evidence-based care and strategic partnerships to address the maternal healthcare crisis in the U.S.

News

Funding and Financial Growth

  • June 2024: Pomelo Care announced a $46 million Series B funding round, bringing its total funding to $87 million. This funding is intended to enhance partnerships with payors and expand the company's reach in addressing maternal health challenges (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-06-20).

Acquisition of The Doula Network

  • September 2024: Pomelo Care acquired The Doula Network, the largest network of credentialed doulas in the U.S. This acquisition allows Pomelo to offer a hybrid care model that combines virtual and in-person support, significantly expanding access to high-quality maternity care (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-09-24).

Clinical Impact and Publications

  • December 2024: Pomelo Care published peer-reviewed data demonstrating the significant impact of its virtual maternal health care model. The data showed a 46% reduction in emergency department utilization and a 58% reduction in NICU admissions among patients engaged in its program (Pomelo Care, PR Newswire, 2024-12-11).

Pomelo Care is positioned as a leader in the maternity care sector, leveraging its unique hybrid model and strategic partnerships to address the maternal healthcare crisis in the U.S. The company's commitment to evidence-based practices and its growing user base highlight its potential to make a lasting impact on maternal and newborn health outcomes.
