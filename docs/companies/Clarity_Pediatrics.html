About Clarity Pediatrics

Clarity Pediatrics is a telehealth service specializing in the diagnosis and treatment of Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) for children and teens aged 5 to 17. The company was founded on November 1, 2021, and operates primarily in California and Texas, providing virtual care without waitlists and accepting insurance (Crunchbase, 2025). Clarity Pediatrics aims to ensure that children with ADHD can thrive by offering accessible and affordable care, addressing the needs of approximately 10% of American children diagnosed with this condition.

The company operates as a platform connecting users with independent medical practitioners, rather than functioning as a traditional medical group or healthcare provider. This model allows Clarity Pediatrics to maintain a broad network of healthcare providers across the United States, although its services are limited to states where they are authorized to operate (Clarity Pediatrics, Privacy Policy, April 2024).

Clarity Pediatrics generates revenue through its telehealth services, which include initial ADHD screenings, behavioral parent training, medication management, and various behavioral therapy programs. The company serves families with children aged 5 to 17, focusing on those affected by ADHD. Its services are distributed through virtual consultations, making it a scalable model within the U.S. market (Clarity Pediatrics Programs Overview, October 2024).

In terms of financial growth, Clarity Pediatrics raised $10 million in funding on April 22, 2024, to expand its virtual pediatric ADHD platform (Business Wire, 2024-04-22). This funding supports the company's mission to reimagine pediatric chronic care, starting with ADHD, by building a tech-enabled virtual clinic backed by scientific research (Crunchbase, 2025).

Key Personnel

The company is led by CEO Christina LaMontagne, who emphasizes the importance of affordable and connected care for families. Product Developments

Clarity Pediatrics has recently updated its offerings to include specialized programs such as RUBI Parent Training for families of children with ADHD and Autism Spectrum Disorder, and Managing Big Emotions, a program designed to help caregivers support children in managing emotional challenges (Clarity Pediatrics Programs Overview, October 2024).
