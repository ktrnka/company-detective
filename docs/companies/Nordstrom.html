About Nordstrom

Nordstrom, Inc. is a prominent American luxury department store chain, originally founded in 1901 as a shoe store. Over the years, it has expanded its offerings to include a wide range of products such as clothing, accessories, and beauty products. The company operates more than 350 locations, including Nordstrom, Nordstrom Rack, and Nordstrom Local stores, and is headquartered in Seattle, Washington. Nordstrom has a significant online presence, ranking No. 21 on Digital Commerce 360’s Top 1000 list of the largest online retailers in North America, with projected online sales of $4.61 billion in 2024 (Duckett et al., Bloomberg, 2024-09-10).

Nordstrom primarily operates in the B2C market, targeting consumers looking for luxury and high-quality products. The company generates revenue through its physical retail locations and its online platform. In Q2 2024, Nordstrom reported net sales of $3.8 billion, with digital sales accounting for 37% of total revenue (Duckett et al., Bloomberg, 2024-09-10).

The company has evolved significantly over time, adapting to changes in consumer preferences and the retail landscape. It has focused on enhancing its digital capabilities and optimizing its supply chain to improve customer service and reduce costs (Carranza, Retail Dive, 2024-09-09).

Third parties often describe Nordstrom as a resilient retailer facing challenges from off-price competitors and shifts in consumer behavior. The company is seen as having a strong brand but is also navigating structural challenges in the traditional department store segment (DiPalma, Yahoo Finance, 2024-09-06).

Key Personnel

  • Erik Nordstrom: CEO of Nordstrom, Inc. Erik is a fourth-generation leader of the company and has been instrumental in steering the company through challenging times. He emphasizes customer service and adapting to market changes as key strategies for the company's success (DiPalma, Yahoo Finance, 2024-09-06).

  • Peter Nordstrom: President of Nordstrom, Inc. Alongside Erik, Peter is involved in the strategic direction of the company and is part of the family-led proposal to take the company private (Duckett et al., CBS News, 2024-09-04).

News

Going Private Proposal

On September 4, 2024, the Nordstrom family, led by Erik and Peter Nordstrom, submitted a $3.76 billion proposal to take the company private, offering $23 per share for the remaining shares they do not already own. This is their second attempt, following a failed bid in 2017. The proposal is under review by a special committee of independent directors (Duckett et al., CBS News, 2024-09-04).

Financial Performance

In Q2 2024, Nordstrom reported a 3.4% increase in net sales, reaching $3.8 billion. Digital sales accounted for 37% of total revenue, up from 36% the previous year. Nordstrom Rack stores showed strong performance with an 8.8% sales increase. However, net income decreased by nearly 11% to $122 million (Duckett et al., Bloomberg, 2024-09-10).

Supply Chain Optimization

Nordstrom has improved its supply chain operations, achieving a 5% increase in click-to-delivery speed and lower fulfillment costs. This led to the decision to halt plans for a new omnichannel center in the Pacific Northwest (Carranza, Retail Dive, 2024-09-09).

Strategic Partnerships

The Nordstrom family is partnering with El Puerto de Liverpool, a Mexico-based retailer with a 9.6% stake in Nordstrom, to finance the proposed buyout. Liverpool's resources and market presence are seen as strategic assets in this endeavor (Duckett et al., Bloomberg, 2024-09-10).
